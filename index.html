About Me

My name is Jeth Gomez. I am a second year honors student at the University of Connecticut, double majoring in Computer Science and Engineering and Mechanical Engineering. I am very interested in robotics and want to combine my love for software with my problem-solving mechanical knowledge and curiosity to create robots and projects that help make the world a better place.

Focus

As humans, we create tools to make our lives easier, and push our capabilites further than what we could do previously. We stay curious, and we are always on the hunt for new knowledge. To be that much closer to understanding the universe.


This is my focus. I am dedicated to creating, designing, and engineering a path to a better tommorow. As a kid, I have always been interested in automation. From learning about the Rube Goldberg Machine to seeing my first robot, I was fascinated by the idea that you can make something that does tasks for you. Going into Computer Science, this was what I was looking forward to the most. Creating software that automates simple but tedious tasks, and making the human experience easier.

Goal

My goal is to leave a lasting impression on the world. Even if they do not know my name or face, I hope that one day I will make a positive contribution to society. I would like to work in a field that not only pushes the boundaries of technology, but also gives back to the community by creating products that are genuinely helpful to the people around us.

Interests

Robotics, embedded systems, mechanical design, AI-assisted engineering, and building systems that interact with the physical world.
